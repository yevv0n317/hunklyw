Lipstick and social change
Color symbolism

1920s   In the 1920s, lipstick symbolized rebellion and independence. It became a hallmark of the flapper era, representing women's liberation, modernity, and breaking traditional norms.

1940s   In the 1940s, lipstick became a symbol of resilience and patriotism during wartime. Women wore it to boost morale, projecting confidence and strength while contributing to the war effort.
Bold red lips were seen as a sign of femininity and defiance, challenging traditional roles and showing solidarity with the shifting societal dynamics of the era.

1960-1970s   Lipstick holds dual significance in feminism. Some reject it as a symbol of imposed beauty standards, resisting societal pressures. Others embrace it as a tool of self-expression and empowerment, highlighting autonomy and choice. This duality makes lipstick both a symbol of oppression and liberation, depending on perspective.
